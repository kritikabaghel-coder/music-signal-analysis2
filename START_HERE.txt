🔊 CONCEPTS EXPLAINED BRIEFLY
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

FFT (Fast Fourier Transform):
  Breaks audio into frequencies
  Shows you which frequencies are loud

STFT (Short-Time Fourier Transform):
  FFT applied at different times
  Creates spectrogram (time + frequency)

Mel Spectrogram:
  Frequencies scaled like human ear
  Lower frequencies more detailed

MFCC (Mel-Frequency Cepstral Coefficients):
  13 numbers that represent sound
  Used in speech recognition systems

Spectral Centroid:
  "Brightness" of the sound
  High = bright, Low = dark

Zero-Crossing Rate:
  How often signal crosses zero
  High = noisy, Low = tonal

Chroma Features:
  Which musical notes are present
  12 pitch classes (C, C#, D, ...)
